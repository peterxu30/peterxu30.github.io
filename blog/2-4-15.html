Musical Beginnings

2/4/15

This semester I'm taking a DeCal, a student taught course, called The Art of Erhu where we learn how to play the erhu, also known as the Chinese violin. I just had my first class on Monday and got my instrument all set up.

I've always known about the erhu but never really had much interest in it until now. Not gonna lie, it was the Legend of Korra soundtrack and its use of the erhu that really got me intrigued. I never knew the instrument could sound so beautiful. Luckily for me, when I googled "erhu berkeley", there just happened to be a beginner class offered at Cal.

I don't expect to be a master by the end of the semester; if I can hold it properly by the end, I consider that a victory. Nonetheless, it is great to pick up an instrument and try something new. College is a time to just get out there and experiment, a lesson I didn't really learn last semester. For now, I must memorize Chinese music notation in preparation of the playing techniques to come next class.

Lastly, for all you poor souls out there who have yet to be blessed by the glory that is the Legend of Korra, give this song a listen:
soundcloud.com/jeremy-zuckerman/the-legend-of-korra.
It's the song that started this whole adventure for me.
